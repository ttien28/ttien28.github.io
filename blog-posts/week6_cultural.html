Cultural Blog: "Stereotype Threat"

September 11th, 2014

What is stereotype threat?

By definition, stereotype threat is a situation in which people are or feel themselves to be at risk of confirming negative stereotypes about their social group.

What are its consequences?

In education, it can also lead students to choose not to pursue the domain of study and, consequently, limit the range of professions that they can pursue. Therefore, the long-term effects of stereotype threat might contribute to educational and social inequality.

My Experiences

Whether I want to believe this or not, being raised in Asian culture comes with the set of beliefs that I have to somehow excel at everything I do when it comes to education and career. But don’t count on me solving me your math problems...I hate math. I was always ehhhh about science. Yes, I do play a musical insturment, but hell, I suck at sports. So yeah, I’m not your typical Asian kid. I always wondered why I couldn’t be like my sister, or the other engineers, teachers or doctors that I had in my household. This in turn, led me to believe that I had a fixed growth mindset I that was so sure I couln’t get out of. But I did. And this process took a lot of self-discovery.

I know that I’ll face this "stereotype threat" and imposter syndome at DBC but I have to realize that I can’t let these things get to me, and that I’m here for a reason. That is to try my hardest to learn and grow alongside everyone else in my cohort. It will be a difficult journey but worth every step.
